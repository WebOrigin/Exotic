Premature Ejaculation (PE)

Premature ejaculation is defined as persistent or recurrent ejaculation with minimal sexual stimulation before, upon, or shortly after penetration. This condition is traditionally diagnosed when sperm release occurs two minutes or less into the sexual act, or even before penetration.

It's very important to understand that most men occasionally reach orgasm sooner than they'd expect.

Lack of ejaculatory control is probably one of the most common male sexual dysfunctions.

It is believed that premature ejaculation is usually caused by emotional or psychological factors. Experts now know that biological factors also play an important role in premature ejaculation.

Premature ejaculation can have a significant impact on many aspects of a man's life. It can affect his and his partner's sexual satisfaction and their ability to build and maintain relationships and can impact a man's general sense of self-confidence.

Premature ejaculation affects between 27 percent and 34 percent of males of all age groups. By contrast, erectile dysfunction only affects an estimated 10 to 12 percent of all men, usually in the older age group.
